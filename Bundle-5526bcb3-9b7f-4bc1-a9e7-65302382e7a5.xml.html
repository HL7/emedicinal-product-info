          <code>

            <status value="additional"/>
            <div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><b>1. What Monuril is and what it is used for</b> Monuril contains the active substance fosfomycin (as fosfomycin trometamol). It is an antibiotic that works by killing bacteria which can cause infections. Monuril is used to treat uncomplicated infection of the bladder in women and female adolescents. Monuril is used as antibiotic prophylaxis for transrectal prostate biopsy in adult man.</div>

          <title value="2. What you need to know before you take Monuril"/>
          <code>
            <coding>
              <system value="https://spor.ema.europa.eu/"/>

            <status value="additional"/>
            <div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><b>2. What you need to know before you take Monuril</b><p><b>Do not take Monuril if you:</b></p><ul><li>are allergic to fosfomycin or any of the other ingredients of this medicine (listed in section 6).</li></ul><b>Warnings and precautions</b> Talk to your doctor, pharmacist or nurse before using Monuril if you suffer from one of the following disorders:<ul><li>persistent infections of the bladder,</li><li>previously had diarrhea after taking any other antibiotics.</li></ul><p><u>Conditions you need to look out for</u></p>Monuril can cause serious side effects. These include allergic reactions and an inflammation of the large intestine. You must look out for certain symptoms while you are taking this medicine, to reduce the risk of any problems. See “Serious side effects” in Section 4.<p><b>Children and adolescents</b></p>Do not give this medicine to children less than 12 years of age, as its safety and efficacy have not been established in this age group.<p><b>Other medicines and Monuril</b></p>Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking, have recently taken or might take any other medicines, including medicines obtained without a prescription. This is especially important if you are taking:<ul><li><b>metoclopramide</b> or other medicinal products that increase the movement of food through the stomach and intestines, because they may reduce the uptake of fosfomycin by your body,</li><li><b>anticoagulants</b>, as their ability to prevent your blood from clotting might be altered by fosfomycin and other antibiotics.</li></ul><p><b>Monuril with food</b></p>Food may delay the absorption of fosfomycin. Therefore, this medicinal product should be taken on an empty stomach (2-3 hours before or 2-3 hours after a meal).<p><b>Pregnancy, breast-feeding and fertility</b></p>If you are pregnant or breast-feeding, think you may be pregnant or are planning to have a baby, ask your doctor or pharmacist for advice before taking this medicine.<p>If you are pregnant your doctor will only give you this medicine when it is clearly needed.</p><p>Breast-feeding mothers can take a single oral dose of this medicine.</p><p><b>Driving and using machines</b></p>You may experience side effects, such as dizziness, which may affect your ability to drive or use machines.<p><b>Monuril contains</b></p><u>Sodium</u> This medicine contains less than 1 mmol sodium (23 mg) per sachet that is to say essentially 'sodium-free'. <u>Sucrose</u> If you have been told by your doctor that you have an intolerance to some sugars, contact your doctor before taking this medicinal product. <u>Sulphites</u> May rarely cause severe hypersensitivity reactions and bronchospasm.</div>

            <div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><p><b>3. How to take Monuril</b></p>Always take this medicine exactly as your doctor or pharmacist has told you. Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you are not sure.<p>In case of treatment of uncomplicated infection of the bladder, in women and female adolescents the recommended dose is 1 sachet of Monuril (3 g Fosfomycin).</p><p>When used as antibiotic prophylaxis for transrectal prostate biopsy the recommended dose is 1 sachet of Monuril (3 g fosfomycin) 3 hours prior to the procedure and 1 sachet of Monuril (3 g fosfomycin) 24 hours after the procedure.</p><p><b>Use in patients with renal impairment</b></p>This medicine should not be used in patients with severe renal impairment (creatinin clearance &lt; 10 ml/min).<p><b>Use in children and adolescents</b></p>This medicine should not be used in children less than 12 years of age.<p><b>Method of administration</b></p>For oral use.<p>Take this medicine by mouth, on an empty stomach (2-3 hours before or 2-3 hours after a meal), preferably before going to bed after emptying the bladder.</p><p>Dissolve the content of one sachet in a glass of water and drink immediately.</p><p><b>If you take more Monuril than you should</b></p>If you accidentally take more than your prescribed dose, contact your doctor or pharmacist.<p>If you have any further questions on the use of this medicine, ask your doctor or pharmacist.</p></div>

            <status value="additional"/>
            <div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"><p><b>4. Possible side effects</b></p>Like all medicines, this medicine can cause side effects, although not everybody gets them.<p><b>Serious side effects</b></p><p><b>While taking Monuril, if you develop any of the following symptoms you should stop taking the medicine and contact your doctor immediately:</b></p><ul><li>anaphylactic shock, a life threatening type of allergic reaction (<em>the frequency is unknown</em>). Symptoms include a sudden onset of rash, itching or hives on the skin and or shortness of breath, wheezing or difficulty in breathing,</li><li>swelling of the face, lips, tongue or throat with breathing difficulties (angioedema) (<em>the frequency is unknown</em>),</li><li>moderate to severe diarrhea, abdominal cramps, bloody stools and/or fever may mean that you have an infection of the large intestine (antibiotic-associated colitis) (<em>the frequency is unknown</em>). Do not take medicines against diarrhea that inhibit the bowel movements (antiperistaltics).</li></ul><p><b>Other side effects</b></p><u>Common (may affect up to 1 in 10 people):</u><ul><li>headache</li><li>dizziness</li><li>diarrhea</li><li>nausea</li><li>indigestion</li><li>abdominal pain</li><li>infection of the female genital organs with symptoms like inflammation, irritation, itching (vulvovaginitis).</li></ul><u>Uncommon (may affect up to 1 in 100 people):</u><ul><li>vomiting</li><li>rash</li><li>urticaria</li><li>itching</li></ul><u>Not known (frequency cannot be estimated from the available data):</u><ul><li>allergic reactions.</li></ul><p><b>Reporting of side effects</b></p>If you get any side effects, talk to your doctor or pharmacist. This includes any possible side effects not listed in this leaflet. You can also report side effects directly via the national reporting system website: <a href="www.mhra.gov.uk/yellowcard">www.mhra.gov.uk/yellowcard</a>. By reporting side effects you can help provide more information on the safety of this medicine.</div>
