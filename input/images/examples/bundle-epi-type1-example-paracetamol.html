1. What Paracetamol is and what it is used for

Paracetamol belongs to a group of medicines called analgesics (painkillers) and antipyretics (medicines to reduce fever).

It is used for the relief of mild to moderate pain and to reduce fever.

2. What you need to know before you take Paracetamol

Do not take Paracetamol: if you are allergic to paracetamol or any of the other ingredients of this medicine.

Warnings and precautions: Consult your doctor before taking this medicine if you have liver or kidney problems.

3. How to take Paracetamol

Always take this medicine exactly as described in this leaflet or as your doctor has told you.

The standard dose for adults is 1-2 tablets (500mg-1000mg) every 4 to 6 hours. Do not exceed 8 tablets in 24 hours.
